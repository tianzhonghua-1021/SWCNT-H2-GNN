Fix the adsorption free-energy equation urgently. The manuscript currently writes G
ads=Gcomplex−(GSWCNT−GH2), which is likely wrong; it should normally be Gcomplex−GSWCNT−GH2. This is the most serious point because it affects the target values.
The ONIOM partition is questionable for Ti-doped CNTs. H₂ is treated at the DFT level, while the SWCNT/Ti region is treated with UFF. This weakens any claim about Ti d-orbitals, Kubas interaction, or Ti–H₂ electronic coupling.
The method may be weak for H₂ physisorption. B3LYP/6-31+G(d) with UFF is used, but dispersion correction is not clearly stated. Reviewers may question the accuracy of H₂ adsorption on carbon surfaces.
The “experimental validation” is too strong as written. The comparison is against real CVD SWCNTs with different purity, defects, bundling, and pressure range, while your model is based on ideal finite CNT structures. It should be reframed as a qualitative comparison unless stronger quantitative validation is added.
The conversion from θ to adsorption volume needs justification. Reviewers may ask why the number of carbon atoms is treated as the number of adsorption sites.
The dataset is structurally small. Although many rows are generated from temperatures and pressures, the actual structure library contains only 44 SWCNT systems, with functionalization and Ti doping mainly at 10 Å.
Random-split results are inflated by data leakage. Random splitting allows similar structures/conditions in train and test sets, so the near-perfect RF/XGBoost results should not be presented as real generalization.
The GNN generalization claim is weak. The manuscript claims good robustness, but structure-based extrapolation is poor; this must be discussed honestly and not hidden behind random-split performance.
Predicting θ may be too formula-driven. Since θ is calculated from ΔG, temperature, pressure, and the Langmuir equation, reviewers may ask why the model does not predict ΔGads first, then derive θ.
Uncertainty quantification needs numerical evidence. Add coverage probability, interval width, number of bootstrap models, calibration method, and clarify that test data were not used for calibration. The SI mentions 20 bootstrap models but leaves some training details incomplete. SHAP and IG interpretations are overclaimed. They should be presented as model explanations, not direct proof of mechanism. Kubas interaction needs stronger proof. HOMO/LUMO, ESP, and ELF are useful, but add Ti–H distance, H–H elongation, charge transfer, NBO/PDOS/EDA, or adsorption energy evidence.
The SI has unfinished parts. Equations and symbols are missing in the Langmuir section, and there are incomplete phrases such as “the is the predicted target.”
Section references and formatting must be corrected. Some main-text references to SI sections do not match the actual SI organization.
Language editing is necessary. There are grammar and wording problems such as “Porous materials has,” “CNTs has,” “substitued,” “tradictional,” and “hydrogen was put outside CNTs.”
